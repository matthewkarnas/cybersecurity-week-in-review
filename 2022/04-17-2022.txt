CISA has added 3 vulnerabilities to it’s known exploited vulnerabilities catalog in the past week based on evidence that threat actors are actively exploiting the vulnerabilities listed. 
https://www.cisa.gov/uscert/ncas/current-activity/2022/04/19/cisa-adds-three-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog

There are 1800 CVEs so far for the month of April 2022. 
https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/full-listing/2022/4

Security advisory/updates released for Dell, Oracle, Drupal, Cisco, Delta Electronics, Ubuntu, HPE, Johnson Controls Metasys SCT Pro, IBM, Microsoft Edge, Siemens, Google Chrome

FBI has released a Flash report detailing indicators of compromise (IOCs) associated with attacks involving BlackCat/ALPHV, a Ransomware-as-a-Service that has compromised at least 60 entities worldwide. 
https://www.ic3.gov/Media/News/2022/220420.pdf

CISA, FBI, and the U.S. Treasury released a joint Cybersecurity Advisory that details cyber threats associated with cryptocurrency thefts and tactics used by a North Korean state-sponsored APT actor Lazarus Group. 
https://www.cisa.gov/uscert/ncas/alerts/aa22-108a

The cyber authorities of US, Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and UK released a joint Cybersecurity Advisory to warn orgs that Russia’s invasion of Ukraine could expose organizations both within and beyond the region to increased malicious cyber activity from Russian state-sponsored cyber actors. https://www.cisa.gov/uscert/ncas/current-activity/2022/04/20/russian-state-sponsored-and-criminal-cyber-threats-critical
